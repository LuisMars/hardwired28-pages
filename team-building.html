4_01_solo.md

Team Building

The Departments have discovered something: collaboration works. Not between Departments, of course. Between contractors. Field operations run smoother when expendable assets coordinate. Fewer replacement costs. Higher retrieval rates. Better quarterly projections. Human Capital ran a pilot program. The survivors reported improved outcomes.

Solo contractors work alone. One operative, full authority, zero witnesses. The Departments use solo assets for sensitive extractions. Deniability increases. Collateral decreases. Subscription costs drop when you only have to pay one survivor.

The Mercenary Registry predates the current Department structure. Eight specialists maintained by Revenue Operations. They cost nothing upfront. They take percentages. They die permanently. Replacements are expensive. They unlock sequentially. The Registry controls the pipeline.

Adapt or be replaced.

SYNERGY THROUGH VIOLENCE. COLLABORATION THROUGH FEAR.
Human Capital Recruitment Poster

Solo Operations

You against the Complex. No backup. No second opinions. Standard warband (1 Boss, 4 Resources), standard objectives, hostile opposition on every front.

Activation Tokens

Solo play uses the standard activation bag with one modification: hostiles activate individually rather than as groups.

Your Warband: Add 1 token per model (5 tokens total).

Hostiles: Add 1 token per hostile model. Each hostile has its own token in the bag.

Token Removal

Remove tokens for Out of Action models at the start of the next round. A model that goes Out of Action mid-round may still have its token drawn (skip the activation).

Hostile Behavior

Hostiles use behavior tables from Inhuman Resources. Each hostile activates independently when its token is drawn.

Solo Campaign

Track Evidence, Leads, and the Post-Game Sequence normally. Standard campaign victory.

Scenario Selection

Roll d3 for tier, d3 for scenario. Or play scenarios sequentially.

Scenario Adaptation

In Aggressor/Defender scenarios, you are always Defender. Follow Defender placement and deployment rules. During deployment, spawn one hostile group in the Aggressor zone (centered). Scenario threats spawn normally.

Cooperative Operations

Multiple players, one warband, pure cooperation. You share resources, share blessings, share the blame when things go wrong.

Team Size

Players Models per Player Total Warband
2 3 6
3 2 6
4 2 8

Model Assignment

Before each mission, divide models among players. Each player controls their assigned models for the entire mission. The player controlling the Boss makes tie-breaking decisions.

Activation Tokens

Friendly Models: Add 1 token per model. When a token is drawn, the player controlling that model activates it.

Hostiles: Add 1 token per spawn group (standard activation rules from Inhuman Resources).

Campaign

The team shares Evidence, Leads, warband roster, and subs balance. All players must be present for each mission. Standard Post-Game Sequence and campaign victory.

Roll d3 for tier, d3 for scenario. Or play scenarios sequentially.

Scenario Scaling

Scale hostile spawns based on warband size.

Total Models Hostile Modifier
6 +1 model per spawn
8 +2 models per spawn

In Aggressor/Defender scenarios, you are always Defender. During deployment, spawn one hostile group in the Aggressor zone (centered).

4_03_mercenaries.md

Mercenary Ladder (Optional)

The Departments maintain a registry of independent contractors. Not freelancers. Specialists. They cost nothing to hire. They take a percentage of everything you recover. They do not gain experience. They already know everything worth knowing. They cannot be fired. They never formally joined.

When they die, they stay dead. The registry updates. The next name becomes available. Revenue Operations controls the pipeline. They always have another name ready.

Hiring Rules

Timing: Hire between missions only.

Limit: 1 mercenary per squad.

Cost: Free. Mercenaries take their cut directly from mission payouts.

Roster Slot: Mercenaries count against your personnel limit. A squad with a mercenary has 4 regular models plus 1 mercenary.

The Ladder

Mercenaries unlock in sequence. You start with access to the first mercenary on the ladder. When that mercenary dies, the next one becomes available.

  1. Hire the first available mercenary (or choose not to hire)
  2. When your current mercenary goes Out of Action, they die
  3. The next mercenary on the ladder becomes available
  4. Repeat until all eight are dead

Once all eight mercenaries are dead, no more are available for that campaign. Use them wisely.

Mercenary Rules

No Experience: Mercenaries do not earn Merits. They cannot be promoted.

No Injuries: If they go Out of Action, they die. No recovery roll.

Cannot Be Fired: Mercenaries leave when they die. Not before.

Fixed Loadout: Equipment cannot be changed, sold, or transferred.

Mode Compatibility

Mercenaries work in all modes. In cooperative play, the team shares the mercenary ladder. In versus play with hostiles, each warband has its own ladder.

4_04_optional.md

Optional Rules

The Departments never stop innovating. New protocols emerge from Resource Allocation's endless efficiency studies. Some squads adopt them. Most don't survive long enough to notice the difference.

Heat

The Departments track contractor activity. Effective squads attract attention. Attention attracts reinforcements.

Track Heat on a scale of 0 to 6. All campaigns start at Heat 0.

Gaining Heat

At the end of any mission where you eliminate all hostile models: gain 2 Heat.

Losing Heat

At the start of each mission: lose 1 Heat (minimum 0).

Heat Effects

Heat Effect
0-2 No effect
3-4 All hostile spawns get +1 model
5-6 All hostile spawns get +2 models

Versus Play with Hostiles

When both warbands play with hostile spawns, share one Heat tracker. Either warband eliminating all hostiles increases Heat.

"Performance metrics indicate elevated contractor efficiency in Sector 7. Recommend asset reallocation."
Resource Allocation Internal Memo

Trophies

Revenue Operations tracks kills. High-value eliminations mean bonus payouts. The Departments call it incentive alignment.

At the end of each mission, gain subs for confirmed eliminations:

Target Bonus
Hostile leader (highest Health in group) 3 subs
Hostile with special ability 2 subs
Standard hostile 1 sub

Only your squads's kills count. Hostiles eliminated by other hostiles or hazards pay nothing.

Escalation

The Complex learns. Hostiles adapt to repeated contractor incursions. Survival breeds resistance.

Track campaign victories. After every 3 victories, hostiles escalate:

Victories Escalation
3 Hostiles spawn with +1 Health
6 Hostile leaders gain +1 DT to all stats
9 All hostile spawns get +1 model

Escalation is cumulative. At 9 victories, hostiles have all three bonuses.

"Quarterly losses exceed projections. Recommend enhanced asset deployment."
Security & Compliance Threat Assessment

Campaign Finale: The Source

At 9 Evidence, the first warband to arrive decides: one more mission for standard victory at 10, or risk everything on extraction now. The signal led here. Six hundred years of sealed doors in the deepest Roots. The Departments sent teams before. None returned. Now you know what they buried.

The Source broadcasts on frequencies that predate the Complex. Predate the Tethering. Predate everything the Departments built their authority on. Carry it out and the Departments lose control. Carry it out and someone finally knows the truth.

Terrain

The Roots. Collapsed infrastructure, emergency lighting. Debris blocks direct paths.

Deployment

Extractor: One model holds the Source. Deploys within 6" of one table edge.

Interceptor: Deploys within 6" of the opposite table edge. In solo or cooperative play, leave empty.

Hostiles: Elite spawn at table center after deployment.

Threats

  • Round 2+: Normal spawns from Extractor's deployment edge
  • Round 4+: Elite spawns from exit edge

The Source

Model holding the Source suffers -1 DT to Wits, and cannot use Broadcast Powers.

If holder goes Out of Action, any model within 1" picks it up (Interact). Otherwise, it drops in place.

Detonation: At game end, the Source explodes. All models within 3" take d6 damage (ignores armor).

Extraction: Extractor exits the opposite table edge with the Source. Campaign victory.

Interception: Interceptor holds the Source at game end or eliminates all Extractor models. Extractor loses d6 Evidence and all Leads. Interceptor captures the Source. At 9 Evidence, the Source holder becomes Extractor.

Failure: Source lost to hostiles or no friendly models remain. Lose d6 Evidence and all Leads. The Departments bury the signal. Again.
